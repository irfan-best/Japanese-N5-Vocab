Sumimasu -> can be used for future tense
sundeimasu -> present tense

irashai -> friendly welcome, friends welcome to their home
irashaimase -> polite used by workers when we are entering the shop.

goro vs gurai: (chatgpt)
    goro -> for around time
    gurai -> about, approximately for quantities, amount, durations, distances, frequencies

yasumimasu vs yasumi o shimasu:
    yasumi o shimasu -> to take a rest
    yasumimasu -> to take a rest / take a day off / be absent

    yasumimasu -> is more often used.

N wa arimasu ka vs N wa arimasen ka:
    N wa arimasu ka -> you don't have any strong expectation of whether the answer is yes or no. 
                    You are simply looking for information.

    N wa arimasen ka:
        This is a negative question, and it is more polite and considerate when making a request. By asking "don't you have it?", you are making it easier for the other person to say "no" without feeling guilty. It can also imply you hope or expect it might be there.
